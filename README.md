# Clothing Product Sales Analysis using SQL

## üìå Project Overview
This project analyzes clothing product sales data using SQL to extract meaningful business insights related to revenue performance, promotions, seasonality, pricing strategy, store placement, materials, and sourcing countries.

The goal is to demonstrate strong SQL fundamentals along with a business-oriented analytical approach.

---

## üìÇ Dataset Description
The dataset contains product-level sales information, including:
- Product name and product type
- Price and sales volume
- Promotion status
- Product position (Aisle, End-cap, Front of Store)
- Season and seasonal indicator
- Section (MAN / WOMAN)
- Material type
- Country of origin

Data cleaning was performed to remove records with missing critical fields such as product name or description.

---

## üîç Key Business Questions Answered
- Which product types contribute the highest percentage of total revenue?
- Which individual products (SKUs) generate the highest revenue?
- How does revenue vary across seasons and gender sections?
- Do promotions significantly impact sales volume and revenue?
- Which store positions drive higher sales performance?
- Are high-priced products compensating lower volume with higher revenue?
- Which materials and origin countries contribute most to sales and revenue?

---

## üìä Key Insights
- Jackets are the **dominant revenue driver**, contributing over half of total revenue.
- A small number of **top SKUs generate disproportionately high revenue**.
- **Autumn and Winter** are the strongest seasons in terms of sales.
- WOMAN section products show **higher revenue and stronger promotional response**.
- Promotions primarily increase **sales volume**, not always revenue.
- High-priced products sell fewer units but generate **higher revenue per SKU**.
- Material choice and sourcing country significantly influence performance.
